This story takes place from 27.05.21 to 31.05.21.

Arjan arrived early in the morning at LaGuardia. I picked him up and we drove back to Larchmont with the plan of him working from my desk until we left for JFK, being driven by PeterJacob at around 1600. At American's stunning JFK Terminal 8, we recognized there was a flaw in our plan. The required Covid test for entry needed to be checked by a check-in agent, and the line was massively long. A group of Milan-bound passengers reached out to an agent, and we were thankfully pulled to the front of the line. However, that was not the end of our problems. Well, my problems.

My test was not time-stamped, but it was taken earlier that day. It was inside 48 hours for that reason, but the agent had a little trouble understanding that. Luckily, I was able to convince her, and then went on to breeze through security and board our 777-200.

## Milan

It was the next morning when we deplaned in Milan. We proceeded through the airport for our on-arrival covid test, and went to the Malpensa Express as soon as we received our respective negative tests. This train went directly to Milano Centrale, the main station in Milan. From there, we took the 3 subway to Duomo and it's surrounding mall, a large plaza in central Milan.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/20210528_085833617_ios.jpeg?w=768)

From there, we walked around the block to Caffe Carlino, where we ordered a pastry and a Cappuccino. Back at Milano Centrale, we struggled to find a train ticket to Florence, but we fortunately did find one on Italotreno, which differed from the Trenitalia we initially were looking for.

The train was unfortunately delayed, which threw a wrench into my work from home plans. However, it did not matter in the long run.

## Florence

Upon arriving in Florence, we made the quick walk past the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore, aka Duomo di Firenze, and arrived at our Airbnb which had an exquisite view. We ran off to get dinner near Mercato Centrale, and I had the most incredible Lasagna. I then ran (literally) to the room in order to take a meeting. Half an hour later, I slammed my laptop closed and we strolled across the Ponte Vecchio to find some Gelato.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/screen-shot-2022-01-02-at-1.18.39-pm.png?w=778)

The next morning, we woke up incredibly early. 9 am certainly hurts with a five hour timezone change. On the way to Galleria dell'Accademia, the home of the David, we stopped at a cafe. At the Gallery, there was a collection of Stradivari instruments, part of a larger rare instruments exhibit. Other renaissance artwork was pretty, but the David stole the show. We then departed in search of another morning snack.

We went toward Mercato Centrale yet again, and discovered that the food court was closed, although the market was open. It was incredible to see Florentines shopping as a part of their daily life. En route to the Uffizi gallery, we did some shopping. Among our favorites was Dan John, home of my Italy pants and Italy shirt.

Now we went to the Uffizi. Something tells me I didn't appreciate it as much as I was supposed to. The interior design, busts, architecture and paintings were amazing, but how many crucifixions can someone look at in one day! After the Uffizi, we had a mediocre lunch (Trattoria Antico Fattore) and better gelato. We then explored Florence by foot, marveling at the architecture. We then found an okay restaurant with a nasty waiter, which was across the street from a worse-looking but better reviewed restaurant of cheeses. However, we found an excellent bar with 5 euro cocktails and delicious Tiramisu. It was called Rooster. To bed we went.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/20210529_064922140_ios.jpeg?w=768)

The next morning we attempted to wake up at 7:30, but again woke up at 9. We trekked over to the Pitti Palace and the Boboli Gardens, on the other side of the Arno.  The garden was an excellent way to start the day, with beautiful greenery, nice paths and an excellent view of Florence from atop a hill. We bid farewell to our Airbnb and began to walk around with our bags.

We went out of the way to a closed restaurant, which was frustrating yet a blessing in disguise. We stumbled upon Trattoria Giovanni and had the most excellent meal of the trip. Giovanni lived in New York and talked to us as we enjoyed Sausage Crostini, Parpadelle with Boar, Wine, and more Tiramisu! We shopped for a moment before leaving Florence en route for Roma on Trenitalia.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/20210530_081306016_ios.jpeg?w=768)

## Rome

After quick deliberation, we decided to take an Uber from Roma Termini to our hotel in Piazza Navona. We then began here at 5pm and did the express Rome sightseeing tour.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/20210530_172910095_ios.jpeg?w=768)

First stop was Piazza Navona. That building in the background is not only a church but was also our hotel! (Eitch Borromini)

After visiting the Colosseum we went to a delicious restaurant, Taverna Fori Imperiali Roma, and begrudgingly walked back to the hotel to call it a night!

The next morning, would you be surprised if I told you we had to run for our train? A delayed Uber led to barely making our train to Fiumicino, the Leonardo Express. We were placed in Business class for the return, which was a first for me! Ten hours later in Dallas, I enrolled in global entry and freaked out about my flight to Detroit, but made it with ease!
