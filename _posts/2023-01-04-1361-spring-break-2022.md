Welcome to my retelling of my fabulous trip to Spain in late February and early March of 2022. I traveled with Drew and Max Davis to visit Jacob in Barcelona, with a special guest appearance from Arjan.

## 24.Feb.2022

To start off, Anna drove us to the airport very early. The drive was easy and we immediately went to the Delta check-in counter, where there was no line. After getting our boarding passes, I split off to the Pre-check line and Max and Drew went through normal security. Once inside, I snuck some cookies and snacks out of the Delta lounge for everyone to enjoy. We then sat and talked at the gate before boarding our flight. Unfortunately, I was in a middle middle seat between a man who was the father of a student that I IA'd with Prof. Krishnan and a woman who had been doing calisthenics at the airport gate. We figured she must've been nice and loose.

Our flight took off for Paris – Charles de Gaulle, and I immediately slept. I missed dinner but woke up in time for an egg McMuffin before landing. We landed early and immediately headed for transfer security and immigration. Fortunately, there were no lines for either, and we had time to explore the very beautiful terminal 2F. We explored the smoking lounge and the red velvet colored carpet, and then boarded our AirFrance flight to Barcelona.

## 25.Feb.2022

We purchased a five day metro pass, and then headed down to the spotless metro station. A train arrived, and we boarded, heading to Europa Fira where we would then transfer to Plaza Espana. From there, we would take the metro to Poble Sec, which is where we were bound to meet Jacob.

We met Jacob at The Student Hotel (TSH) before we headed to Esfane Istanbul for some excellent cheap Turkish food. I got some sort of pita and my favorite cheap beer, Estrella Damm. We then purchased SIM cards, more is to come from this. It is excellent and was a staple of our trip. Jacob showed us his room and we dropped off our bags. From there we took the metro to Placa Catalunya, where we walked around and explored the Gothic Quarter. Some highlights of this walk were the Barcelona Cathedral, La Rambla and the Mercado de La Boqueria.

Initially we pivoted to touring Sagrada Familia, but we were unable to get tickets. Instead, we met up with Jacob and went to Arenas de Barcelona, an old bullfighting stadium, to get lunch. Lunch was tasty, and we set out for Montjuic. To get there, we first took a funicular from Paral-lel, and then took Teleferic de Montjuic, a cable car. This was a very scenic journey.

Once atop the mount, we walked around and basked in the glory of the Port of Barcelona. It was magnificent. On top of Montjuic there is a castle, and that was where we headed next. We thought we needed to pay for tickets and turned away, but we were told that it was free and headed up. The castle was cool, giving 360 degree panoramas of the entire city and Mediterranean sea.

## 28.Feb.2022

Finally, we were headed to Montserrat. To do this we headed to Plaza Espana, which is where we would catch the commuter rail. This was the first thing that we did, and it would occupy most of the day.

We got off the train at Aeri de Montserrat, and took another cable car (I know I love cable cars) to the top of the mountain. It was a perfectly clear day, and the mountain and it's peaks shined in the sunlight. Truly, it was breathtaking. Once atop the mountain, well technically about halfway up, we walked around the town. We saw the "Stairway to Heaven" and I tried some local cheese at a street market. Sadly, we had to eat in a cafeteria-style restaurant, but it was still enjoyable due to Jamon y Queso, my favorite sandwich. After this, we hiked for a long time to many different spots.

The first stop was a cross where there were locks chained to the fence with inscriptions. After sitting here for a moment, we continued up for what we thought would only be a short while. However, Max Davis kept pushing us, and we climbed a lot more than any of us expected to. Luckily, the views were stellar and we got some great pictures.

Drew, Jacob and I hiked down, but Max continued to go up. Once back at the town, we headed into the Monastery. It was very pretty, and had the "Black Madonna," a famous statue. Max joined us and we headed back down in the cable car, and back to Barcelona on commuter rail.

## 01.Mar.2022

This morning, we woke up early at 06:00. We immediately hiked to the Diagonal metro station, our home base, and got on the blue line to Barcelona Sants, the regional rail station. After arriving at the station there was security that we had to go through. I then bought a croissant and orange juice, and boarded the train. I was sitting with Arjan, and Max and Drew sat together. Jacob did not join us to Valencia because he had been the previous weekend.

On board the train I watched TV, slept and got to look at the Spanish coast along the Mediterranean Sea. We arrived at Valencia at 10:00. We walked out of the train station (Joaquin Sorolla) and headed toward the main Plaza, which is where our hotel was. The hotel [Plaza Venecio Central] was incredible, with nice beds, a nice shower, and incredible location in the heart of Valencia. We were awarded early check in and dropped our bags off.

After checking in, we headed to a cafe called Federal. It was located in a beautiful yellow building. Max did his own thing for breakfast, and Arjan, Drew and I stayed here. I split delicious pancakes with Arjan, and had a great Cortado as well. It was fantastic.

There were even overpasses from the old river that passed above. It was a great walk, on a beautiful day. We walked through the park on the way to the City of Arts and Sciences.

The City of Arts and Sciences was stunning, with very interesting modern-white architecture. We walked into the museum of sciences to check it out, and ultimately ended at L'Oceanografic, the largest aquarium in Europe. We saw some spectacular animals, including penguins, Beluga whales, sharks, flamingos and a dolphin show.

## 02.Mar.2022

In the morning, Arjan awoke early. He was headed to the airport in order to catch a flight back to London. After all, he was in the middle of his study abroad session! This left me alone in Valencia. What does one do when left in a foreign city by their self. Go for a walk!

My walk began in central Valencia. The first stop on my walk was the Valencian Mercat Central, a grandiose market in the center of the city. There was plenty variety of merchants, ranging from textiles to Jamon y queso. After stopping in the market, I went across the street to La Lonja de la Seda, which is an old mercantile market from the 15th century. The architecture is stunning, with large spiral columns inside, as well as a column with plentiful orange trees.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/7420c2a7-1f19-40ec-970b-e0d892e987df_1_105_c.jpeg?w=1024)

I then headed to the metro to head for the beach. In order to reach the beach from the center of the city, I had to take the subway to the end of the line, and then take the tram from there to the coast. The metro was very easy to use and very clean, and I would certainly use it again. The Valencian beach was an incredibly wide swath of sand, with the occasional sunbather stretched over a blanket. The beach formed the shape of a crescent, going out to the left and out to sea, as if it were never ending.

After walking on the beach, I grabbed a bocadillo from a cafe and headed back to the city. As I emerged from the subway, I heard loud fireworks and helicopters overhead. It didn't take long for me to realise that I had missed the Fallas demonstration for the day. Fallas is a large annual celebration in Valencia, and surely I will have to return and experience it in the future.

I began to walk to Valencia Joaquin Sorolla station in order to catch the return train to Barcelona, hoping I would arrive by 6 or 7. The train ride was on time and pleasant, dropping me in Barcelona just in time to meet everyone to head to L'illa Diagonal, a large shopping mall.

The mall was very stereotypical, only differing from those in America by the shop composition. The centerpiece of the mall was a large Zara. I indulged myself and bought a bomber jacket, longsleeve shirt, and pants. While at the mall, a plan was made to go to the club Bling Bling. Before heading to the club, we stopped at Jacob's dorm to drop off our purchases.

At the club we were rewarded with a great surprise. One of the girls in Jacob's study abroad friend group was celebrating their birthday and somehow secured a table with free bottle service for everyone. They even brought her a cake and sang happy birthday inside the club! Jacob, Drew, Max and I danced the night away listening to mostly American music, which was a nice upgrade from the Techno offerings at Apolo.

The cathedral was absolutely stunning, both inside and outside. The outside of the cathedral towers over the neighboring area and has very ornate details, all masterfully designed by Antoni Gaudi. The inside, while equally if not more impressive, is much different. Colored light shines through the plentiful stained glass windows, illumating every part of the cathedral in a different way. This lent itself to incredible pictures and sights.

Although this should be the end of the night, it is more accurately the beginning of the next day…

Our flight from Barcelona to Paris was at 6:30 am so we planned to arrive at the airport at around 3:30. The only sensible way to fill the time was to go out!

We started the night back where we started our trip-at the George Payne Pub! If I recall correctly Max D and Jacob left Tierney, Drew and I to search for some weed. Fortunately, I ran into Asher and we still had a nice time. After George Payne, we headed to Cocovail. Cocovail is the quintessential study abroad bar, with flags from almost every university in the US adorning their walls. We had a few pints and then headed back to the Generator to pick up our belongings before heading to the airport.

## 05.Mar.2022

We got to the airport at around 3:30 only to find out that the checkin line wouldn't open till 4. At 4, we were forced to check our bags due to them being over 12kg. After heading through security we went to McDonald's to get some breakfast. McDonald's truly is better in other countries. What a shame.

We boarded our flight to Paris and arrived in time to transfer to the M gates for our flight back to the US. There was one last hiccup before we boarded though. Max D went to the bathroom without any means of communication or way to know the time, and was in there for an incredibly long time. Drew and I worried that he didn't realize how close to boarding we were, and that he (and us too) would miss the flight despite being at the gate. Fortunately, he turned up and we boarded the plane.

The flight was nice… No complaints. Alec picked us up from DTW and we all went to sleep almost immediately due to both the jetlag and the all nighter.

The trip to Barcelona with the 1361 roomies was one that I will always remember. It helped to prove that a trip truly can cross off many items, such as traditional sightseeing, day trips, overnight trips, partying and visiting a friend. I would highly recommend a trip to Barcelona for all, as there truly is something for everyone.
