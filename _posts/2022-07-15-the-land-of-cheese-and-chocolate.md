Bonjour, and welcome to the best weekend trip I've ever embarked on. This story begins on June 30th at the New Rochelle train station. I boarded the Amtrak train en route to Philadelphia, where I would meet Arjan and work from his brothers apartment before our long, and I mean long trip to Interlaken.

In the midst of a quiet day at Etsy, Arjan and I went to Honeygrow for lunch. I liked it so much that I stopped back here in August after returning from Spain! We then took the SEPTA to the airport, had an easy check-in with a lot of time, and were awarded business class tickets! Some nice relaxing and eating occurred, and we landed in London.

Our stop at Heathrow was very complicated, to say the least. Don't worry, this isn't foreshadowing future trouble here at all! Anyways, after deplaning, we made our way to the BA checkin desk. We were traveling to Switzerland the first week that it opened for vaccinated Americans, and BA's system didn't give us permission to check in. To quote myself "checkin agent didn't know we were allowed to go."Thank god we had our documents in order. After showing them the updated guidance and a phone call, we were given boarding passes.

Now comes the Heathrow praise. Terminal 5 is beautiful and has great dining and shopping. It really felt like London. I went to the Harry Potter store, Ted Baker and Fortnum and Mason!

From there we took a train from the airport to the Geneva central train station, and walked a few blocks to Lake Geneva. Fortunately the Jet d'Eau was spewing water when we arrived, which was quite a site. We hurried back to the train station and caught a train bound for Bern, the first stop on our long journey. The train was nice and the views were spectacular. At Bern we got off the train, went into a tunnel, and then got into another train. So yes, I have been to Bern on a technicality.

Once on board the Interlaken Ost-bound train, we were awarded with even more amazing views of Thunersee and the beginning of the Swiss Alps. Once we arrived, we transferred once more to the 103 bus to Bonigen, and got off at Bonigen Dorf. From there, we walked about 50 meters to the Swiss Kiwi Chalet, our Airbnb.

After settling down, we headed back to Interlaken proper and had dinner at a Bierhaus. I got a mega-brat and it was incredible. We then walked from East to West where we caught a bus to take us back home. I fell asleep immediately and put rest to a long day of traveling.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210701_182447424_ios.jpeg?w=768)

*The MegaBrat*

Guten tag and welcome to German Swiss, home to a fantastic day of travel. I woke up early with no alarm, and an extremely refreshing sleep. We were off to Schilthorn, Piz Gloria, home of Bond world. Our first stop was Lauterbrunnen valley. Well, actually it was the Coop in the train station, but that's not important. The train to Lauterbrunnen ascended us through the valley, bringing us past raging rapids and waterfalls. When we got off the train, it was breathtaking.

We got breakfast outdoors, walked around the river, and hiked into a grassy hill where we took pictures and had our Sound of Music moment. After this, we began our trek up Schilthorn to Piz Gloria and James Bond world.

The first mode of transportation was a cable car. After this, we took the train to Murren, which is an incredibly beautiful town and certainly worth stopping by. From there we made our way up to the top.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210702_073650272_ios.jpeg?w=768)

*Lauterbrunnen*

The top of the mountain was stunning. Although it was cloudy, there were moments where they subsided to reveal the vastness of the Alpine landscape. We walked around the peak, and I went off on a quick hike while Arjan explored the museum. We then were seated for our reservation at the rotating restaurant.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210702_090633271_ios.jpeg?w=768)

*Murren*

This was definitely a cool experience, but the food was nothing spectacular. The martini was incredibly strong, which was a plus. Or maybe it was just the altitude…

We went back down the mountain and stopped at Brig (or Birg), where there was an incredible view and accompanying cliff walk.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210702_114947017_ios.jpeg?w=768)

Down we continued, traveling via Lauterbrunnen to get back to Interlaken. In Interlaken we went for an E-bike ride to Thunersee. It was an easy and beautiful ride.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210702_143110871_ios-1.jpeg?w=768)

*Me*

We then checked out the Bonigen beach club since the EURO semi-final match was on. We grabbed dinner at Little Thai, which was pretty good, before heading to Barracuda to watch the remainder of the game, where Switzerland lost in PK's. After this we decided to walk back to the beach club, where there was live folk music being played, it was wonderful.

Since I'm struggling to write this all, I will switch to more of a itinerary style of writing:

07/03/21

1. Took the train to Grindelwald first thing in the morning after grabbing a snack at Backeri – Konditorei Feuz.

2. In Grindelwald walked around and actually played Mini-Golf! Absolutely stunning views up there in the mountains.

3. Back to Interlaken for lunch before heading to Bonigen one last time. Ate at the 3a which was under a decent looking hostel.

4. Headed to Bonigen for our ferry service to Brienz via Riggenberg, Iseltwald, and Giessbach See. This ferry was very nice and not too expensive.

5. Once in Brienz we boarded the train to Luzern. This cog-wheel train was incredible and took us through the mountains past many lakes.

6. Arrive in Luzern and check into Hotel Des Alpes, which had a stunning view of the Chapel bridge and Luzernersee.

7. We walked in the pouring rain to Pastarazzi which was really tasty. I took the long way home and walked along the north shore of the lake and through the old town before ending up at Mr. Pickwick Pub, where the England vs Ukraine game was being shown.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210703_090045884_ios.jpeg?w=768)

*Grindelwald*

07/04/21

1. Early morning we headed to the Luzern Bahnhof to catch our train to Zurich. This train was quick and still had some nice views of the country. From Zurich HB we took the train to Stadelhofen and disembarked.

2. We checked into Hotel Europe and then headed to Universitatsspital to get our Covid tests to return to the US. Arjan left his phone at the checkin of the hotel and had me freaked out.

3. We then walked past the opera house and headed to what we thought was Bahnhofstrasse. Anyways, we missed the stop and ended up at Badenerstrasse. German.

4. Lunch was had at Nooch Asian Kitchen, which was phenomenal and totally made up for the misdirection.

5. We decided on some hot chocolate and scratched this itch at Cafe & Conditorei 1842, which had a stunning interior.

6. We then headed to Kilchberg to the Lindt home of chocolate. I was slightly freaked since they messed up the Covid test encryption but we figured it out. Lindt was incredible. We got to see the history of the company, how chocolate is made, and got to try different chocolates.

7. We also did the Chocolatier class which was the highlight of the museum. We were able to pour and decorate our own chocolate bars all while donning the Lindt Chocolatier outfit!

8. We headed back to Zurich where we walked around the neighborhood that our hotel was in before heading to the north along the river.

9. We ate at Restaurant Mere Catherine which had nice fish, before walking around one last time.

![image](https://maxliving847609587.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/20210704_092259095_ios.jpeg?w=768)

*Opera House*

07/05/21

1. Early morning airports are never fun. We took the first tram to Zurich HB and hopped on a train to Zurich Flughaven for our 7AM flight to Madrid. The airport was easy and I have no complaints.

2. Madrid is a crazy airport. Especially during covid. And especially transferring between terminals. We landed and had to go through Covid check

3. Then had to find and get on the bus to T4.

4. Had to go through security and then get on a train

5. Went through passport control

6. Arjan had to run to the gate and I had extra time

7. I boarded early but our cockpit door wouldn't shut, so we were on the ground for an hour before departing. Thank god for business class!

07/06/21

1. Back in the US I landed at JFK T8 and took the Airtrain to Jamaica where I was picked up by my parents who were returning home from Eastport.

2. We ate chocolate and I was happy to be home in time for work!
